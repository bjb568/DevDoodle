Headers

If you look at a newspaper, you'll most likely notice the big black letters that spell out the headline. In HTML, these are called headers; they're quite useful.

"H" is for Header

In an article in the paper there are multiple levels of headings. There's the article title, sub-headings for the article, sub-sub-headings for the sub-headings... let's try not to get too creative here. So how would we write those in HTML? Well, we use an "h" for header then a number from 1-6, where 1 is the most important and highest in the hierarchy (rendered as the largest in your browser), and 6 means the least important and lowest on the hierarchy (rendered as the smallest in your browser). In general, there should be 1 level-1 header (<h1>) per page and only a heading of one level lower rank following another heading.

<h1>I'm going to be big and important! :D</h1> <h6>I'm going to be tiny and detailed. :(</h6>

To move on, write all the possible header tags from largest to smallest, and in each one, put "Smaller".
