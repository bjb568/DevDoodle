Welcome to DevDoodle

DevDoodle is the a free universal repository of programming knowledge for programmers of all languages.

Our community strives to make the internet a better place by crafting answers to questions that will have a lasting impact on as many people as possible.

We help with learning new programming languages thru community-provided tutorials and lessons.

Have fun sharing your creations with the world and learn from constructive feedback.

Quality

To facilitate our goal of creating content that is useful to the greater community, we have very strict quality standards. This is not a discussion forum: All posts should be on-topic and helpful to as many people as possible.

  • Upvote helpful and interesting posts to increase their visibility and signal that they're useful.
  • Downvote unhelpful or harmful posts to decrease their visibility and discourage similar behavior. If they can be improved, consider suggesting improvements by commenting or editing yourself.
  • Flag posts with serious problems so they can be dealt with by privileged users.

Programs that primarily serve a non-programming related purpose will be removed.

Editing

Questions and answers can be edited by the community to correct mistakes. We encourage editing whenever you see a fixable problem with a post.

Tho programs can't be directly edited, they can be forked to add functionality. Forking a program creates an editable clone of it owned by the forker.

Community

Almost everything that is done on DevDoodle is a community process. The community casts votes on site policies and moderation of posts, community members help each other by solving their problems and collaborating on projects, and users can posts feature requests on meta to add site functionality for the benefit of the community.

  • Respect other's time by cutting out noise from your posts.

Commenting

Comment on any post to ask for more information or suggest improvements. You can always comment on your own post, but you need 50 reputation to comment on others'.

Try to avoid extended discussion in comments: use a chatroom instead.

Moderation

The community self-moderates by reviewing post quality and improving salvageable content. Common moderation tasks are divided into queues which focus on specific goals such as improving content, correcting user behavior, and evaluating content.

  • Overall content quality is improved thru recategorization, broadening, and correcting language, technical, and formatting issues.
  • Behavior rules are enforced by the community.
  • Low quality posts are evaluated and dealt with in a democratic way where multiple reviewers cast their vote.

In this way, the community is able to keep a high quality standard with minimal moderator intervention.

Privileges

As users become familiar to the site's functionality, practices, guidelines, and culture, privileges are granted for communication, reward, and moderation. Reputation is gained from votes on your posts.

  • All users can ask and answer questions, post programs, and access lessons.
  • Users with 10 reputation can post on meta.
  • Users with 15 reputation can vote.
  • Users with 20 reputation can use generic flags.
  • Users with 30 reputation can chat.
  • Users with 50 reputation can comment anywhere.
  • Users with 50 reputation can use custom moderator flags.
  • Users with 75 reputation no longer have their posts peer-reviewed.
  • Users with a history of helpful edits no longer need to have their edits peer-reviewed.
  • Users with a history of helpful flags gain access to review queues.
  • Users with a history of good reviews can cast deletion/undeletion votes and change protection states.
  • Users with an active involvement with the site and a history of good judgment can privately communicate with other users and suspend users.
  • Users who have unlocked all privileges can run for moderator in a democratic election.
